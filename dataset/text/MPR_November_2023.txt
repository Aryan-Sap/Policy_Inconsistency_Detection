Monthly Policy Review
November 2023
Highlights of this Issue
GDP grows at 7.6% in second quarter of 2023-24 (p. 2)
Growth in all sectors was positive in the second quarter of 2023-24 with manufacturing sector registering the highest
growth rate at 13.9%, followed by construction and electricity sectors.
Cabinet approves Terms of Reference of the 16th Finance Commission (p. 4)
The Commission will provide its recommendation for a period of five years beginning April 1, 2026. Its
recommendations will provide for the distribution of tax proceeds between the central government and states.
Standing Committee submits report on Bills seeking to replace three criminal laws (p. 2)
Key recommendations include: (i) replacing unsound mind with mental illness, (ii) redrafting the provision on petty
organised crime, (iii) granting bail to undertrial prisoners, and (iv) clarifying the admissibility of electronic evidence.
Cabinet extends the Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana by five years (p. 5)
The extension will come into force from January 1, 2024. The scheme provides about 81 crore beneficiaries with free
foodgrains. These include rice, wheat, and coarse grains/millet.
Regulations governing Foreign Higher Educational in India, released (p. 6)
Regulations delineate: (i) eligibility criteria for foreign HEIs, (ii) the process for obtaining approval to set up campuses
in India, and (iii) powers of foreign HEIs to offer programmes, levy fees and admit students in India.
Comments invited on the draft Broadcasting Services (Regulation) Bill, 2023 (p. 5)
The draft Bill seeks to regulate broadcasters and broadcasting network operators including OTT platforms. A three-
tier structure for regulation of content has been proposed.
Rules under the Van (Sanrakshan Evam Smavardhan) Adhiniyam, notified (p. 8)
The Rules specify procedure and timeline for approval of projects by the central government, compensatory
afforestation, and initiation of proceedings against offences.
Draft National Pharmacy Commission Bill released for consultation (p. 7)
The Commission will regulate the education and practice of pharmacy. The Bill seeks to replace the Pharmacy Act,
1948.
The Model Prisons and Correctional Services Act, 2023 released (p. 3)
The Model Act provides for classification and segregation of prisoners, constitution of an Undertrial Review
Committee in every district, and security and surveillance measures for prisons.
RBI increases risk weights for certain loan categories (p. 4)
The revised risk weights would apply primarily to unsecured consumer loans such as personal loans. Risk weights
determine the minimum amount of capital that lending entities must hold as per risk profile of a loan.
Cabinet approves continuation of scheme for Fast Track Special Courts (p. 8)
The scheme was implemented to provided dedicated court machinery to victims of sexual crimes. It has been
extended for three years, with an outlay of Rs 1,952 crore.
Digital Advertisement Policy 2023 released (p. 6)
The Policy lays out the framework for advertisements by the government on digital media platforms. These include
OTT platforms, social media platforms and digital audio platforms.
