Monthly Policy Review
January 2025
Highlights of this Issue
Budget Session of Parliament commences on January 31 (p. 2)
The Budget Session of Parliament commenced on January 31. The session is scheduled to have 27 sitting days,
and end on April 4, 2025.
President addresses joint sitting of Parliament (p.2)
The President outlined the major policy achievements and objectives of the government in her address. These
covered sectors such as governance, agriculture, and health.
Economic Survey 2024-25 released (p.2)
The Survey has estimated real GDP growth of 6.3%-6.8% in 2025-26, expected to be supported by sustained
investments, improvement in consumer confidence, rural demand, and pick-up in corporate wages.
GDP estimated to grow by 6.4% in 2024-25(p. 3)
India’s GDP is estimated to grow by 6.4% in 2024-25 (at constant prices), as per the first advance estimates
released by the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation.
Cabinet approves Eighth Pay Commission for central government employees (p.3)
The Union Cabinet approved the constitution of the Eighth Central Pay Commission for central government
employees. The term of recommendations of the Seventh Pay Commission will conclude in 2026.
Consumer Price Index inflation was 5.6% in the third quarter of 2024-25 (p. 3)
CPI inflation was 5.4% in the third quarter last year, while it was 4.2% in the second quarter of 2024-25. Food
inflation in the third quarter of 2024-25 was 9.4%.
UGC issues Draft Regulations for appointment of faculty and VC in HEIs (p. 4)
The Draft Regulations allow appointing a VC from amongst persons from the industry. They also allow
recruiting assistant professor from amongst persons with only a master’s degree in engineering.
Supreme Court strikes down domicile reservation in PG medical courses (p. 5)
The judgment mandates that domicile-based reservations violate fundamental right to equality before law. It
ruled that PG medical admissions must be based on merit.
JPC submits report on the Waqf (Amendment) Bill, 2024 (p. 5)
Recommended changes include: (i) providing that waqf by user will be invalid prospectively, and (ii) replacing
the collector, responsible for identifying government property, with another officer chosen by state government.
Ministry invites comments on Draft Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025 (p. 6)
The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 grants the government authority to establish rules. The Rules
contains provisions related to data fiduciary obligations, consent management, and breach notifications.
Aadhaar Authentication for Good Governance Rules, 2020 amended (p. 6)
Aadhaar authentication may also be used for the purpose of promoting ease of living of residents and enabling
better access to services. Non-government entities may also use Aadhaar authentication for specified purposes.
