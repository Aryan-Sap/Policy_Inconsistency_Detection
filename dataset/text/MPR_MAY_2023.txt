Monthly Policy Review
May 2023
Highlights of this Issue
Supreme Court gives control over services in Delhi to Delhi government (p.2)
The Supreme Court ruled that the legislative and executive power over the officers of IAS and Joint Cadre
Services in Delhi government shall lie with the Delhi government.
Ordinance to amend the Government of NCT of Delhi Act promulgated (p.3)
The Delhi Legislative Assembly will not have the power to legislate on the subject of ‘services’. Transfers and
postings will be decided by a three-member committee, two of whom are appointed by the central government.
GDP grew by 6.1% in the fourth quarter of 2022-23 (p.2)
GDP for 2022-23 is estimated to grow by 7.2% in 2022-23 as compared to 9.1% in 2021-22. This is marginally
higher than the GDP growth of 7% as estimated in the Second Advance Estimates.
RBI withdraws Rs 2,000 denomination banknotes from circulation (p.4)
Rs 2,000 denomination banknotes will continue to be legal tender. People may deposit such banknotes in their
bank accounts or exchange them into banknotes of other denominations by September 30, 2023.
Industrial production grew by 4% in the fourth quarter of 2022-23 (p. 2)
The Index of Industrial Production (IIP) grew by 4% in the fourth quarter, compared to an increase of 1.8% in
the corresponding quarter of 2021-22. In the third quarter of 2022-23, IIP had increased by 2.8%.
SC holds that the legislature party acts on the direction of the political party (p.3)
The Supreme Court gave its verdict over the party split and disqualification of Maharashtra MLAs. It ruled that
the political party, not the legislature party, appoints the whip and leader of the party in the House.
Cabinet approves PLI scheme for IT hardware (p.5)
The Union Cabinet approved PLI Scheme 2.0 for IT hardware. The estimated expenditure under the scheme is
Rs 17,000 crore over six years, and expected investment is Rs 2,430 crore.
Accessibility standards for healthcare facilities notified (p.7)
The standards prescribe requirements for hospitals and other healthcare facilities, for inpatient and outpatient
facilities and medical equipment.
CEA notifies National Electricity Plan for 2022-32 (p.6)
Target capacity addition for 2017-2022 could not be achieved mainly due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
Additional coal capacity required till 2031-32 may vary from 19,000 MW to 27,000 MW.
Amendments to Green Energy Open Access Rules notified (p.7)
The amendments redefine eligibility criteria for consumers eligible for the open access framework and exempts
offshore wind projects from cross-subsidy and additional surcharge.
Fertiliser subsidy rates revised: Rs 38,000 crore will be provided for Kharif 2023 (p. 9)
The Union Budget estimated fertiliser subsidy expenditure at Rs 1,75,100 crore in 2023-24. Cabinet approved
revised rates will apply between January-March 2023 for Rabi and April-September 2023 for Kharif crops.
Amendments to Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products Rules, 2004 notified (p.8)
The amendments require publishers of curated online streaming content to incorporate anti-tobacco advertising
in case of content that features consumption of tobacco products.
