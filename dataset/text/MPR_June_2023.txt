Monthly Policy Review
June 2023
Highlights of this Issue
Current account deficit at 0.2% of GDP in the fourth quarter of 2022-23 (p. 2)
India recorded a current account deficit of USD 1.3 billion (0.2% of GDP) in the fourth quarter (January-March)
of 2022-23, significantly lower than USD 13.4 billion (1.6% of GDP) in the corresponding quarter of 2021-22.
Repo rate kept unchanged at 6.5% (p. 2)
The Monetary Policy Committee of the Reserve Bank of India retained the policy repo rate (the rate at which
RBI lends money to banks) at 6.5%. Other policy rates also remain unchanged.
Cabinet approves third revival package for BSNL (p. 2)
The package provides for allotment of 4G and 5G spectrum to BSNL through equity infusion. The total
projected outlay is Rs 89,047 crore.
Cabinet approves subsidies for fertilisers (p. 3)
Cabinet has approved the extension of the subsidy for urea fertilisers until 2024-25. Sulphur-coated urea will be
introduced in the country. A new scheme for promoting organic fertilisers has also been approved.
FRP for the 2023-24 sugar season approved at Rs 315 per quintal (p. 3)
For the basic recovery rate of 10.25%, the Fair and Renumative Price (FRP) for sugarcane for the 2023-24 sugar
season has been set at Rs 315 per quintal, an increase of about 3% over the previous season.
MSP for Kharif crops enhanced for the 2023-24 marketing season (p. 3)
The Cabinet has approved an increase in MSP for Kharif crops for the 2023-24 marketing season (ranging
between 5%-10%). The MSP for paddy has been increased by 7%.
Time-of-day tariff mandated for retail consumers (p. 4)
The Ministry of Power has amended the Electricity (Rights of Consumers) Rules, 2020. It introduces the time-
of-day tariff for retail consumers (except for agricultural consumers) in a phased manner.
The UGC (Institutions deemed to be Universities) Regulations, 2023 notified (p. 8)
The 2023 Regulations supersede the UGC (Institutions Deemed to be Universities) Regulations, 2019. They
specify the eligibility criteria for the deemed university status.
Changes to Liberalised Remittance Scheme put on hold (p. 7)
In May 2023, the Ministry of Finance had decided to consider international credit card transactions under the
limits of liberalised remittance scheme. It has decided to put the implementation of this measure on hold.
Ministry of Power notifies Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (p. 4)
Under the scheme certain entities shall comply with emission standards notified by Ministry of Environment.
Entities failing to meet their targets can trade in carbon credits to meet emission targets.
Comments invited on draft Green Credit Programme Implementation Rules, 2023 (p. 7)
The Programme seeks to create a market mechanism to create green credits for environment friendly actions.
Such actions include tree plantation and waste management.
The Law Commission invites comments on the Uniform Civil Code (p. 7)
The 22nd Law Commission has invited comments on the Uniform Civil Code from recognised religious
organisations as well as the public at large.
