Monthly Policy Review
July 2024
Highlights of this Issue
Budget Session 2024 begins (p. 2)
The Budget Session of Parliament commenced on July 22, 2024. Five Bills have been listed for introduction,
consideration, and passing. The Bharatiya Vayuyan Vidheyak, 2024 has been introduced.
Union Budget 2024-25 presented (p. 2)
The government proposes to spend Rs 48,20,512 crore in 2024-25, an increase of 8.5% over the actuals for
2023-24. The fiscal deficit is estimated at 4.9% of GDP, lower than the actuals for 2023-24 (5.6%).
Economic Survey 2023-24 released (p. 2)
The Survey has estimated real GDP growth of 6.5%-7% in 2024-25, expected to be supported by strong domestic
investment demand, improved agricultural performance, and an increase in merchandise and services exports.
Bill to replace The Aircraft Act, 1934 introduced in parliament (p. 4)
The Bill seeks to replace the Aircraft Act, 1934. The Act regulates the civil aviation sector. The Bill retains the
regulatory structure under the 1934 Act.
Consumer Price Index inflation was 4.9% in the first quarter of 2024-25 (p. 3)
CPI inflation in the first quarter of 2024-25 was higher than the same quarter last year (4.6%). Food inflation
was 8.9% in the first quarter of 2024-25.
Supreme Court upholds state’s power to tax mines and minerals (p. 6)
The Court held that royalty collected on mining activities is not in the nature of tax. It held that state
legislatures have the power to impose tax on mineral bearing land.
RBI issues directions on treatment of wilful and large defaulters (p. 3)
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) issued the RBI (Treatment of Wilful Defaulters and Large Defaulters)
Directions, 2024. It provides a procedure for the classification of a borrower as wilful defaulter by lenders
Comments invited on draft Digital Bharat Nidhi Rules, 2024 (p. 4)
The draft Rules operationalise the Digital Bharat Nidhi, which was set up under the Telecommunications Act,
2023. The DBN replaces and renames the Universal Service Obligation Fund.
Guidelines for implementing green hydrogen production incentive scheme released (p. 6)
Guidelines provide a framework for selecting beneficiaries for the second tranche of incentive scheme. Green
Hydrogen production capacity of 4,50,000 MT will be allocated.
Comments sought on Draft revised guidelines on EV charging infrastructure (p. 7)
The draft revised guidelines (2024) seek to replace all previous guidelines. The draft provides a comprehensive
framework for constructing and operating EV charging stations in various settings.
Ministry of Education constitutes the Education Advisory Council (p. 4)
The Council will prepare a roadmap for the effective implementation of the NEP. It will consist of eminent
members from academia, philanthropy and industry who are experienced in key areas of education.
